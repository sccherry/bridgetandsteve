Getting here

Reagan National Airport (DCA)

The closest and most convenient airport.

Washington Dulles International Airport (IAD)

Not quite so close or convenient, but they do have buses, shuttles, and cabs.

Baltimore Washington International Airport (BWI)

Also not so close or convenient, but they also have buses, and shuttles; maybe skip the cab.

Amtrak - Union Station

For East Coasters, Amtrak is another excellent option. The train gets into Union Station, which also has the Metro (red line) or is a short 15-20 minute cab ride to the wedding hotels.

While you’re here

Bridget and Steve are big fans of DC and all that it has to offer. Here are some suggestions for your time here. If you need more ideas, don&rsqquo;t hesitate to reach out! We love to share ways to explore our city.

For families and the young at heart

  • The National Zoo: Check out the adorable baby pandas and other animals. Also, it’s totally free (if you take public transit and don&rsqquo;t park there.)
  • imagiNATIONS Activity Center, National Museum of the American Indian: Lots of great activities for kids. Also free.
  • The National Building Museum: Often has unique, hands-on offerings for kids; exhibits change frequently.
  • Local playgrounds: Kalorama Park and Mitchell Park park come highly recommended by Bridget’s niece (and her parents).

For those looking to live like a local

  • Meridian Pint: The bar where Bridget and Steve had their first date. Make your own beer sampler and try the pulled pork sandwich.
  • Glen’s Garden Market: Great for sandwiches on the go and a local beer or two on the patio, if it’s not too crowded.
  • Right Proper: Local beer and tasty food in the heart of the Shaw neighborhood.
  • Heurich House: The former home of DC’s most famous brewer. Fun fact: his brewery once stood where the Kennedy Center now is. Tours offered on Thursdays, Fridays, and Saturdays.
  • Woodrow Wilson House: President Wilson is the only President in recent memory who decided to stay in DC after his time in office. The home and grounds are beautiful and you can learn a lot about a fascinating era in history. Guided tours from Wednesday to Sunday.

For those looking to explore the great outdoors

  • Self-guided monument tour: Get a Capital Bikeshare bike and explore the monuments. Be sure to check out the Jefferson Memorial (Steve’s favorite) and the Lincoln Memorial (Bridget’s favorite). Or take a guided version by signing up for a Segway tour.
  • Hains Point / Gravelly Point: Also using a Bikeshare bike, check out Hains Point Park and/or ride out along the Mount Vernon trail to watch the planes land at Gravelly Point.
  • Georgetown Waterfront: Rent a kayak at the Key Bridge Boathouse to get a view of DC from the Potomac. Afterward, get a snack of macarons at Malmaison nearby.
  • Dumbarton Oaks: The gardens here are amazingly beautiful and well worth the $10 admission fee. If you’re interested in some history—or air conditioning—after the gardens, check out the free museum.
